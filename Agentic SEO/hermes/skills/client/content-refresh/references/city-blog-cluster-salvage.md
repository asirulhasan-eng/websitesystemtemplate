# Salvaging Over-Templated City SEO Blog Clusters

## Lesson captured
When a competitor-gap batch creates many city pages from the same source pattern, do not treat the repeated editorial angle as independent demand. In this case, RankMeTop-style city {{AUDIENCE}} roundup gaps were batch re-angled into `Local Pack Teardown` titles. The better response is to salvage the written assets into a differentiated city strategy cluster, not delete or keep scaling the template.

## Diagnosis checklist
1. Confirm the opportunity source in SQLite, not just Obsidian/task-note mirrors.
2. Compare original brief/title against current title and event history.
3. Count deployed vs preview-ready pages.
4. Inspect live pages for thinness: word count, headings, images, status 200, canonicals.
5. Check whether rank tracking includes the city keywords.
6. Decide whether repetition is a title/framing problem, a content-uniqueness problem, or a noindex/delete problem.

## Salvage strategy
- Keep existing URLs unless there is a strong canonical/redirect reason.
- Rework titles/H1/meta away from one repeated formula.
- Add one genuinely city-specific section to every page.
- Position pages as `city {{NICHE}} SEO strategy` assets, not fake local-pack teardown reports.
- Add a hub-and-spoke internal-link structure such as a `{{NICHE}} SEO by City` hub.
- Use each city post to support main service pages and relevant Google Maps/local SEO guides.
- Add rank tracking before judging success.

## Page roles
- City blog: educational/strategy proof for local market understanding.
- Main service page: conversion page for buyer intent.
- Hub page: index and internal-link authority distributor for the city cluster.

## Preview-ready pages
Do not merge preview-ready city-template pages as-is. Before production:
1. Rewrite title/H1/meta.
2. Add city-specific differentiation.
3. Add internal links into hub/service/support pages.
4. Validate preview and only then merge if quality passes.

## Deployed pages
Do not immediately delete/noindex deployed pages just because the title pattern is bad. First create a semi-safe refresh branch that preserves URLs, improves differentiation, validates 200/canonical/sitemap/internal links, and adds tracking. Consolidate/noindex only after GSC/rank data shows no value.
